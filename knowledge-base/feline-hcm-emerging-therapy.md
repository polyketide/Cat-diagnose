## 0. The one finding, with its limits attached to it

A 2024 study tested the **cardiac myosin inhibitor CK-586** — a small-molecule sarcomere inhibitor, the same drug *class* as mavacamten/aficamten in human medicine — in cats with **obstructive** HCM (oHCM, i.e. with left ventricular outflow tract obstruction, LVOTO). A single oral dose *"safely ameliorated LVOTO"*: it *"dose-dependently eliminated obstruction (reduced LVOTOmaxPG), increased measures of systolic chamber size (LVIDs Sx), and decreased select measures of heart function (LV FS% and LV EF%) in the absence of impact on heart rate"* (Rivas 2024, PMID 38802475).

That is a coherent, mechanistically sensible result: a myosin inhibitor is *supposed* to reduce hypercontractility, so the **fall in FS% and EF% is the intended effect, not a side effect** — the paper's own title says the drug *"minimally reduces systolic function and ameliorates obstruction."* Lowering ejection fraction here is how the obstruction is relieved, and it was dose-dependent and well-tolerated over 48 hours.

> **⭐ Why this is a signal, not a recommendation — the three limits that must travel with it.**
> 1. **n = 6.** *"six purpose-bred cats with naturally occurring oHCM"* — a six-animal pharmacodynamic study, not a clinical trial with owned patients and outcomes.
> 2. **Preclinical, single-dose, 48 hours.** *"A blinded, randomized, five-treatment group, crossover preclinical trial"* measuring *"6-, 24-, and 48-hour"* effects of a **single oral dose**. It shows the drug does what its mechanism predicts over two days; it says nothing about survival, quality of life, chronic dosing, or whether cats live longer.
> 3. **Industry-affiliated.** Several authors are affiliated with **Cytokinetics, Inc.**, the drug's developer (from the author affiliations in the record). That does not make the result wrong — the data are the data — but it is standard-of-reading context for an early efficacy signal from a manufacturer.
>
> The authors themselves frame it as opening a door: the results *"pave the way for the potential use of CK-586."* **Potential. Future. Not yet a treatment you can ask for.**

## 1. What this means at the decision moment

- **There is no action here for an owner today.** CK-586 is investigational; this file records that an early signal exists, so that a circulating "new HCM drug!" post can be met with the actual evidence — one 6-cat, single-dose, preclinical study — rather than hype or dismissal.
- **The finding is specifically about *obstructive* HCM (LVOTO present).** It does not speak to non-obstructive HCM, and it is not a general "HCM cure."
- **If your cat has HCM, the questions that matter are elsewhere.** Screening, staging and standard drugs are a veterinary cardiologist's conversation this file does not substitute for; the acute dangers — sudden hindlimb paralysis/pain (ATE) and laboured breathing (CHF) — are emergencies covered in [`emergency-triage-red-flags.md`](emergency-triage-red-flags.md). **This file is a note on a drug in development, not guidance on managing a cat.**
